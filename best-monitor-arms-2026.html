How to Choose a Monitor Arm

Check Your Monitor's Weight and Size First

This is the most common mistake. Before buying any arm, check your monitor's manual for its weight. Standard 27" monitors weigh 10–15 lbs. Ultrawides can be 20–25 lbs. Make sure the arm's rated capacity exceeds your monitor's weight by at least 20%.

Clamp vs Grommet Mount

Most monitor arms include both clamp (hooks over desk edge) and grommet (bolts through a hole in the desk) mounts. Clamp is easier to install. Grommet is more permanent and stable. For most people, clamp is perfect.

Gas Spring vs Mechanical Spring

Ergotron uses a "constant-force" mechanical spring — this gives it the famous smooth, one-finger repositioning. Cheaper arms use spring mechanisms that gradually lose tension. If you'll adjust your monitor position daily, the Ergotron's spring mechanism is worth the premium.

FAQ

Is a monitor arm actually worth it? +
Absolutely. A monitor arm frees up 15–30% of your desk surface, lets you get your monitor at exactly eye level (reducing neck strain), and allows you to push it out of the way when you need desk space. For anyone who spends 6+ hours a day at a computer, it's one of the highest ROI desk accessories you can buy.
What's the difference between the Ergotron LX and the Amazon Basics arm? +
The Amazon Basics Monitor Arm is manufactured by Ergotron under the Amazon brand. The internal mechanics are virtually identical. The main differences are branding, warranty length (10 years vs 1 year), and finish quality. If you want maximum peace of mind, get the branded LX. If you want to save $40 with near-identical performance, get the Amazon Basics.
Can I use a monitor arm on a standing desk? +
Yes — monitor arms work great on standing desks. In fact, pairing a standing desk with a monitor arm is ideal because you can adjust the monitor height independently of the desk, ensuring perfect eye level whether you're sitting or standing.
