Standing Desk Buying Guide

Single Motor vs Dual Motor

Single motor desks are cheaper but wobble more, especially at full standing height. If you'll use the standing feature seriously (30+ minutes at a time), get a dual-motor desk. The Flexispot E7 Pro is the sweet spot.

Weight Capacity

A typical dual-monitor setup with accessories weighs 30–60 lbs. A 154-lb capacity is fine for most people. Only go higher if you have an ultrawide or triple-monitor setup.

Height Range

Make sure the lowest sitting height works for your body. Shorter users (under 5'4") need a desk that goes down to 27–28". Most standard desks go down to 28–29", which is fine for average height.

FAQ

Are standing desks actually good for you? +
Yes — but not because standing is inherently better than sitting. The benefit is in alternating between sitting and standing. Switching positions every 30–60 minutes reduces chronic back pain, improves circulation, and can increase energy levels. The goal isn't to stand all day — it's to move more.
What's the best standing desk under $400? +
The Flexispot E2W at around $299 is the best under-$400 standing desk. At $399 the FEZIBO L-shaped desk offers excellent value if you need corner desk space.
How long does assembly take? +
Budget for 45–90 minutes for most standing desks. The Vari Electric is the exception at 15 minutes because it ships partially assembled. Have a power drill ready — it speeds assembly up significantly.
