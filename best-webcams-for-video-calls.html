Webcam Buying Guide

1080p vs 4K: Do You Actually Need 4K?

For Zoom, Teams, and Google Meet — 1080p is sufficient and what most platforms stream at anyway. 4K becomes valuable if you record videos for YouTube, stream to an audience, or your company uses a platform that supports higher resolutions. For pure video calls, the C920x at $69 looks great.

Low-Light Performance

This is what most people overlook. A $200 webcam in a dark room often looks worse than a $70 webcam in good lighting. Before buying a premium webcam, improve your lighting first — a simple ring light ($20) can make a $69 webcam look better than a $199 one.

Built-in Microphone Quality

All webcams on this list have built-in microphones, but quality varies significantly. The Microsoft Modern Webcam has the best built-in mic at its price. If your calls sound echoey or distant, consider a dedicated USB microphone — it makes a bigger difference than upgrading your webcam.

FAQ

Is the Logitech C920x still worth buying in 2026? +
Absolutely. It's been the best budget webcam for years and nothing has genuinely dethroned it at the $69 price point. The image quality is solid 1080p, the dual microphones work well, and it has 89,000+ reviews backing up its reliability. If you're upgrading from a built-in laptop webcam, the C920x is the best first step.
Does lighting matter more than webcam quality? +
Yes. Lighting has a bigger impact on how you look on video calls than webcam resolution. A $15 ring light or positioning yourself in front of a window (facing it, not with it behind you) will improve your video quality more than switching from a $70 to a $200 webcam. Fix your lighting first, then consider upgrading your webcam.
What's the best webcam for Zoom specifically? +
The Logitech MX Brio 705 for the best overall Zoom experience, or the C920x for the best budget option. Both are in Logitech's lineup which has native Zoom certification, meaning they work plug-and-play without any configuration.
